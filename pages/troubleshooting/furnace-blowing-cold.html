Furnace Blowing Cold Air? Try These 3 Fixes Before Calling.

There is nothing more frustrating than waking up to a shivering house when the furnace should be roaring. Before you pay for a service call, check these three common culprits.

1. Check the Thermostat 'Fan' Setting

Ensure your fan is set to AUTO, not ON. If it's set to ON, the blower will run constantly even when the furnace isn't heating the air, resulting in a cold breeze.

2. Inspect Your Air Filter

A severely clogged filter restricts airflow. This can cause the furnace to overheat and trigger a safety 'limit switch' that shuts off the burners while keeping the blower running to cool it down.

3. Verify the Pilot Light or Igniter

On older units, the pilot light may have gone out. On newer units, the electronic igniter may be dirty or cracked. If you don't hear the 'click-click-whoosh' of ignition, this is likely the issue.

CRITICAL SAFETY WARNING

If these steps don't restore heat, your heat exchanger might be cracked. This is a severe fire and carbon monoxide hazard. Call Woodall immediately for a safety check.
